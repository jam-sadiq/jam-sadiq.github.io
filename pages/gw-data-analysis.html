Overview

Each gravitational-wave event carries information not only about the two compact objects that merged, but also about the broader population from which they were drawn â€” their mass distribution, spin alignments, and cosmic merger rate. By analysing ensembles of events I can infer the underlying astrophysical distributions and constrain binary formation channels.

My work employs hierarchical Bayesian inference and Monte-Carlo methods to combine posterior samples from individual events into robust population-level constraints, accounting for selection effects and search sensitivity.

Key Activities

Merger rate estimation as a function of redshift, mass-ratio and spin distributions of BBH systems, model comparison between isolated binary evolution and dynamical-capture channels, and development of efficient sampling algorithms for hierarchical models.
