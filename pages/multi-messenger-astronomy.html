Overview

Short gamma-ray bursts (sGRBs) are believed to originate from the merger of two neutron stars or a neutron-starâ€“black-hole binary. These events are expected to also produce detectable gravitational waves. By targeting GW searches to the time and sky position of a reported GRB, we can dramatically reduce the noise background and push searches to greater sensitivity.

The PyGRB pipeline implements this targeted search strategy, coherently combining data from all operating detectors at the time of each GRB trigger to look for an associated GW signal.

Key Activities

Running PyGRB analyses on GRB catalogues from Fermi-GBM and Swift-BAT, developing Bayesian upper-limit methods for non-detections, studying joint electromagnetic and gravitational-wave parameter estimation, and improving pipeline sensitivity through template-bank optimisation.
