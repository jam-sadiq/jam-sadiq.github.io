Research Focus

Waveform Modeling

Building accurate gravitational-wave templates for precessing compact binaries by combining numerical relativity simulations with post-Newtonian analytical approximants.

Overview

Accurate waveform models are the backbone of matched-filter searches and Bayesian parameter estimation. For binaries with misaligned spins, precession of the orbital plane imprints complex amplitude and phase modulations on the gravitational-wave signal that must be captured faithfully to avoid biased parameter recovery.

My work focuses on hybrid waveforms that stitch together the precision of numerical relativity (NR) in the late inspiral and merger regime with the analytical control of post-Newtonian (PN) or effective-one-body (EOB) models at earlier times, specifically for precessing-binary configurations.
